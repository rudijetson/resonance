Executive Summary

What the Community is Saying

North College Hill residents demonstrate deep commitment to their community despite decades of disinvestment. Across all five pillars, a clear message emerges: residents are ready for action, not more surveys.


There is near-universal support (78%+) for community-controlled solutions like land trusts and investment funds. Residents explicitly connect current challenges to historical patterns‚Äîredlining, displacement in Over-the-Rhine, broken promises‚Äîand demand authentic community control over any new initiatives.


The community identifies immediate needs (pharmacy, grocery store, vacant property intervention) alongside long-term vision (Hamilton Avenue transformation, generational wealth building). Critically, trust must be earned through visible quick wins before larger initiatives can succeed.

Key Takeaways

1
Action over surveys - Community is skeptical from past disappointments
2
Community control - Not just input, but genuine governance power
3
Anti-displacement - Fear of being "OTR'd" is explicit and evidence-based
4
Quick wins needed - Visible action builds trust for bigger initiatives
5
Church partnerships - Faith institutions are trusted anchors
Community Priorities

Where to Focus First

Community Land Trust

Partner with churches to record elder stories before they're lost

Create Business Directory

Comprehensive listing with Black-owned business identification

Address Worst Vacant Properties

Visible action on long-standing eyesores builds credibility

Add art vendors, music, make it year-round where possible
