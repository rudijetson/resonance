## 2. Geographic Context

### Location
- Located in Hamilton County, Ohio
- Approximately 10 miles north of downtown Cincinnati
- Named for its proximity to Cincinnati's College Hill neighborhood (formerly Pleasant Hill), which borders it to the south
- Mt. Healthy borders to the north

### Major Streets and Thoroughfares
- **Hamilton Avenue** - Primary commercial corridor running north-south, connecting to downtown Cincinnati
- **West Galbraith Road** - Major east-west thoroughfare
- **Ronald Reagan Cross County Highway** - Regional connector
- **Interstate 75** - Major highway access
- **North Bend Road** - Intersection with Hamilton Avenue

### Historic Neighborhoods
The city originated from three distinct subdivisions incorporated in 1916:

1. **Meyerville** (1905+) - North of Galbraith Road, west of Hamilton Avenue
   - Built by saw mill owner John Meyer using surplus lumber

2. **Clovernook** - East of Hamilton Avenue
   - Named after historic Clovernook Farm

3. **Sunshine** - South of Galbraith, west of Hamilton

**Total population at incorporation:** ~500 residents

### Landmarks and Points of Interest
- **Cary Cottage** - White brick house built in 1832, on campus of Clovernook Center for the Blind and Visually Impaired, listed on National Register of Historic Places
- **LaBoiteaux-Cary Cemetery** - City's oldest landmark
- **Clovernook Center for the Blind & Visually Impaired** - Historic site on former Clovernook Farm
- **Stapleton Park** - Received new play structures and improvements, rededication ceremony held August 25, 2024
- **Marshal Peter Dumele Park** - City-run park
- Six city-run parks total

### Historical Significance
- Underground Railroad connections
- Home to abolition movement activity
- Literary heritage: Alice and Phoebe Cary (notable 19th-century poets) grew up in the area

## 3. Economic Conditions

### Business Landscape
- **Primary commercial areas:** Hamilton Avenue and West Galbraith Road
- **Job opportunities:** Predominantly in restaurant and retail sectors
- **Commercial Façade Improvement Program (CFIP):** Up to $25,000 in federal grant money available to qualifying businesses for façade and street appeal improvements

### Development Projects

#### College Hill Station (Major Development)
- **Location:** Intersection of Hamilton Avenue and North Bend Road
- **Size:** Over 4.5 acres
- **Status:** Transforming long-vacant land into vibrant, mixed-use development
- **Significance:** Key redevelopment initiative for the community

### Vacant Properties
- **Resident reports:** 60% of residents note "a few vacant or abandoned properties scattered throughout the area"
- **Maintenance concerns:** Some vacant lots with grass growing approximately one foot before being cut
- Contributing to visual blight in certain areas

### Property Values
- **Median Home Value:** $125,200 (2023)
  - 12.5% increase from 2022 ($111,300)
  - 54% lower than national average
  - 0.413 times smaller than national average of $303,400

### Community Communication
- City relaunched quarterly newsletter in June 2024
- Includes Mayor's Corner and content from various city departments
- Keeps residents informed about developments and municipal activities

## 4. Housing Situation

### Affordability
- **Median Property Value:** $125,200 (2023)
- **Comparison to National Average:** 54% lower
- **Price Trend:** +12.5% increase from 2022 to 2023
- **Assessment:** "Relatively low property prices compared to other areas, making it an accessible and attractive option for homebuyers and renters"

### Homeownership
- **Homeownership Rate:** 61.3% (2023)
- **Owner-Occupied Households:** 15% lower than national average
- **Renter-Occupied Households:** 27% higher than national average

### Housing Conditions and Concerns
- **Construction Era:** Most houses built around the same time with similar construction
- **Infrastructure Issues:** Many streets don't have sidewalks
- **Community Concerns:** Residents express desire for "more homeownership rather than home rentals"
- **Rental Prevalence:** Higher than national average, affecting neighborhood stability

### Historical Context
- By contrast, neighboring West College Hill had 77% homeownership rate by 1940 (historically Black community developed by Charles M. Steele)

## 5. Community Issues and Recent News

### Major Crisis Events (2024)

#### Fire Department Mass Resignation (March 2024)
- More than a dozen firefighters resigned
- Community protests and supporters walking out of city meeting
- City Attorney criticized fire department for resignations
- **Resident concerns:** "It scares the hell out of you" - fears about service delays if relying on mutual aid agreements
- Significant community tension over public safety

#### Police Chief Termination
- Former police chief filed lawsuit against the city
- Claimed termination was "for no lawful reason"
- Connected termination to age and race discrimination
- Followed complaints about creating hostile work environment (March 2024)

#### School Violence Incident
- Two adults charged with inciting violence, rioting, disorderly conduct, and endangering children
- Followed student fight witnessed by North College Hills City Schools superintendent
- Community seeking solutions to violence concerns

### Community Events and Programs

#### Ongoing Events
- **Food Truck Fridays** - 1st and 3rd Friday of each month, 4:00 PM - 9:00 PM at 1500 W. Galbraith Rd
- **Monthly Community Clean-up Days** - Recycling and neighborhood beautification
- **Juneteenth Jam** - Scheduled for June 2025

#### 2024 Events
- **Stapleton Park Rededication & Ribbon Cutting** - August 25, 2024 at 12:30 PM
  - New play structures and park improvements
- **Memorial for Doug Eikens** - City employee passed away unexpectedly August 15, 2024
- **City Newsletter Relaunch** - June 2024, quarterly publication

### Transportation Development
- **Hamilton Avenue Corridor BRT Project** - Bus Rapid Transit planned for 11-mile corridor between downtown Cincinnati and North College Hill/Mt. Healthy
- Will operate in dedicated bus-only lanes, bus and turn-only lanes, and mixed traffic

## 7. Relationship to Surrounding Areas

### Neighboring Communities

#### College Hill (Cincinnati Neighborhood - South)
- **Relationship:** North College Hill takes its name from College Hill, which borders directly to the south
- **Historical Name:** College Hill was formerly called "Pleasant Hill"
- **Current Status:** Experiencing dining and commercial revitalization
- **Connectivity:** Connected via Hamilton Avenue corridor
- **Surrounding neighborhoods:** Mt. Airy, Winton Hills, Wintondale, and North College Hill

#### Mt. Healthy (North)
- **Location:** Borders North College Hill to the north
- **Connection:** Hamilton Avenue BRT project will serve both communities
- **Historical Context:** Matured as a town during 19th century while land between it and College Hill remained farmland

#### Cincinnati (Downtown - South)
- **Distance:** Approximately 10 miles south of North College Hill
- **Connection:** Hamilton Avenue provides direct route to downtown
- **Regional Context:** North College Hill is considered an inner suburb of Cincinnati
- **Transportation:** BRT project will create 11-mile dedicated transit corridor

### Hamilton Avenue Corridor Importance
- **Function:** Primary commercial and transportation spine
- **Connectivity:** Links North College Hill with College Hill, other northern Cincinnati neighborhoods, and downtown
- **Development:** Focus of commercial activity, dining, and shopping
- **Future:** BRT project will enhance rapid transit access to downtown Cincinnati and regional employment centers

### Regional Context
- Part of Hamilton County
- Access to Ronald Reagan Cross County Highway and I-75 for regional travel
- Through the 19th century, area remained largely farmland between maturing towns of College Hill (south) and Mt. Healthy (north)
- Incorporated in 1916 as population grew and subdivisions developed

### Community Identity
- Distinct municipality separate from Cincinnati
- Own city government, schools (North College Hills City Schools), fire department (historically), and services
- Shares regional identity with greater Cincinnati area
- Part of northern Cincinnati suburban/urban corridor
